## RECITALS

Covered Entity is a HIPAA Covered Entity subject to the requirements of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH), and related regulations.

Business Associate performs certain services for Covered Entity that may involve the creation, receipt, maintenance, or transmission of Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of Covered Entity.

The parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Protected Health Information" (PHI)** means individually identifiable health information as defined in 45 CFR §160.103.

**1.2 "Electronic Protected Health Information" (ePHI)** means PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted in electronic form.

**1.3 "Breach"** has the meaning set forth in 45 CFR §164.402.

**1.4 "Security Incident"** means any attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system.

**1.5 "Subcontractor"** means any person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of, or provides services to, Business Associate that involve the use or disclosure of PHI.

---

## 2. PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES

**2.1** Business Associate may use and disclose PHI only:
- To perform the specific services described in the underlying services agreement
- As required by law
- For Business Associate's proper management and administration, if necessary

**2.2** Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI in any manner not permitted by this Agreement or required by law.

**2.3** Business Associate shall not sell PHI or use PHI for marketing purposes.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**3.1 Safeguards.** Business Associate shall implement and maintain appropriate Administrative, Physical, and Technical Safeguards as required under the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C) to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI.

**3.2 Subcontractors.** Business Associate shall enter into a written agreement with any Subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Business Associate, requiring such Subcontractor to comply with HIPAA requirements equivalent to those in this Agreement.

**3.3 Breach Notification.** Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity without unreasonable delay and in no case later than **72 hours** after discovery of a Breach of Unsecured PHI. Notification shall include, to the extent possible:
- The identification of each individual whose PHI was or is reasonably believed to have been affected
- A description of the Breach
- The types of information involved
- Steps individuals should take to protect themselves
- What Business Associate is doing to investigate, mitigate, and prevent future Breaches
- Contact information for Business Associate

**3.4 Security Incidents.** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any Security Incident of which it becomes aware, including Breaches of Unsecured PHI.

**3.5 Access to PHI.** Business Associate shall make PHI available to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to individuals, as necessary to satisfy Covered Entity's obligations under the HIPAA Privacy Rule.

**3.6 Amendment of PHI.** Business Associate shall make PHI available for amendment and shall incorporate any amendments directed by Covered Entity.

**3.7 Accounting of Disclosures.** Business Associate shall document disclosures of PHI and make such documentation available to Covered Entity to satisfy Covered Entity's obligations to provide an accounting of disclosures.

**3.8 HITECH Compliance.** Business Associate shall comply with all applicable provisions of the HITECH Act and any regulations issued thereunder, including provisions relating to Breach notification and security.

**3.9 Audits.** Upon reasonable notice, Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) or Covered Entity for purposes of determining compliance with HIPAA.

---

## 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

**4.1 Term.** This Agreement shall be effective as of the date set forth above and shall remain in effect until terminated.

**4.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement if the other party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement, provided that the non-breaching party provides written notice of the breach and the breaching party fails to cure within 30 days.

**4.3 Obligations Upon Termination.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Business Associate shall:
- Return or destroy all PHI received from, or created on behalf of, Covered Entity
- Provide written certification of such return or destruction within 30 days
- If return or destruction is not feasible, extend the protections of this Agreement to any remaining PHI

---

## 5. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

Business Associate represents and warrants that it:
- Has implemented HIPAA-required Administrative, Physical, and Technical Safeguards
- Will conduct workforce training on HIPAA requirements
- Will conduct periodic risk assessments
- Has the capability to fulfill all obligations set forth in this Agreement

---

## 6. INDEMNIFICATION

Business Associate shall indemnify and hold harmless Covered Entity from and against any claims, losses, liabilities, penalties, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from Business Associate's breach of this Agreement or violation of applicable law.

---

## 7. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**7.1 Amendment.** This Agreement may only be amended by written agreement signed by both parties.

**7.2 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [State].

**7.3 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the underlying services agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof.
