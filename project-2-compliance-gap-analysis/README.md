# Project 2 — Compliance Gap Analysis

**Scenario:** A SaaS company preparing for ISO 27001 certification and SOC 2 Type II audit engaged the GRC team to conduct a formal gap analysis. The company also wanted to map their posture against the NIST CSF to identify maturity levels across cybersecurity domains.

**Frameworks:** ISO 27001:2022 · SOC 2 Type II (Trust Service Criteria) · NIST CSF 2.0

## 🎯 Objectives

1. Assess current compliance posture against three frameworks
2. Identify control gaps and rate severity
3. Prioritize remediation by effort and compliance impact
4. Build a 12-month roadmap to ISO 27001 certification and SOC 2 Type II readiness

---

## Key Findings Summary

### ISO 27001:2022
- **93 controls assessed** across all Annex A domains
- **41% fully implemented** | **35% partially implemented** | **24% not implemented**
- Biggest gaps: Supplier security, logging & monitoring, incident management

### SOC 2 Trust Service Criteria
- **CC1–CC9 assessed** (Common Criteria) + **Availability (A1)**
- **52% of criteria met** | **31% partially met** | **17% not met**
- Biggest gaps: Change management, risk assessment, vendor management

### NIST CSF 2.0
- Overall maturity: **Tier 2 (Risk Informed)** — target Tier 3 by year-end
- Weakest function: **Detect** (ID.DE) — limited SIEM/monitoring capability
- Strongest function: **Protect** — endpoint controls and access management solid

## Compliance Timeline

```
Month 1–3:   Foundation — Policies, ISMS scope, asset inventory
Month 4–6:   Technical controls — SIEM, patch management, MFA
Month 7–9:   SOC 2 evidence collection begins, internal audit
Month 10–12: ISO 27001 Stage 1 audit, SOC 2 Type II observation period
```
